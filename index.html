Case Study

Instasize

RoleLead Product Designer
Timeline2013 – 2024
PlatformiOS, Android

The Challenge

Instasize started as a simple photo resizing tool. As the market evolved, the product needed to become a full creative suite without alienating the millions of casual users who loved its simplicity. The challenge was scaling features while keeping the core experience fast and intuitive.

The Approach

I led the design team through an iterative process rooted in data and user feedback. We conducted extensive A/B testing across subscription flows and growth loops, redesigned the editing pipeline to support video alongside photo, and built a cohesive design system that unified the experience across both platforms.

Key Decisions

Introduced a modular editing architecture that let us ship features independently per platform. Redesigned onboarding to reduce time-to-value by 40%. Created a subscription model that balanced free-tier engagement with premium conversion.

Introducing AI to a Simple Product

The hardest design challenge came late in the product lifecycle: introducing generative AI capabilities (AI-powered effects, avatar creation, background removal, intelligent enhancement) into a product built on simplicity. The risk was alienating 150M users who came for a fast, predictable editing flow. I protected the core experience by keeping AI features opt-in and progressive. AI tools lived alongside manual controls, never replacing them. Users could explore generative features without losing access to the workflows they already trusted.

Knool

An AI-powered platform helping attorneys analyze and navigate complex case documents, grown to $500K ARR.

AI LegalTech SaaS
Case Study

Knool

RoleFounding Designer
Timeline2024 – Present
PlatformWeb SaaS (AI)

The Challenge

Attorneys spend hundreds of hours manually reviewing case documents. AI could collapse that timeline, but the legal industry is deeply skeptical of AI output, and for good reason: hallucinated case law has already led to sanctions. The product had to earn trust with a user base that is trained to be adversarial toward every claim.

Designing for AI Trust

Every AI response is cited with exact source references from the uploaded case documents, so attorneys can verify before relying on anything. The AI is intentionally constrained to the four corners of uploaded evidence and will not speculate beyond it. I designed this boundary to be visible and understandable, not hidden behind a disclaimer.

The fact-check feature surfaces both corroborating and contradicting evidence, not just confirming results. This was a deliberate design choice: attorneys think adversarially, so the tool needed to match that mental model. I chose a conversational interface over traditional search because the target users are non-technical professionals who needed the lowest possible learning curve to adopt AI tooling.

The Approach

Ran contextual inquiry sessions at three law firms to map real workflows before designing anything. Built a guided onboarding flow that established trust through transparency, showing the AI's reasoning and source material alongside every answer. Designed an information architecture optimized for legal document hierarchies and cross-reference patterns.

The Challenge

Build a family of three creative apps (Made, Videomade, and Selfiemade) that each had a distinct personality but shared recognizable design DNA. Each app targeted a different creative workflow while maintaining a unified brand experience.

The Approach

Developed a shared design system with flexible brand tokens that could adapt per-app. Led brand workshops to define each app's unique character. Crafted a motion design language that carried across the suite, making transitions and micro-interactions feel cohesive.

Key Decisions

Created a modular template system so content creators could start fast. Used gesture-driven editing to minimize UI chrome. Each app used the same core nav pattern but with distinct color palettes and iconography.

FinTech Gamification iOS
Case Study

PaperTrade

RoleProduct Designer
Timeline2019 – 2020
PlatformiOS

The Challenge

Stock trading apps intimidate beginners. We needed to create a space where anyone could learn market mechanics without risking real money, while making it engaging enough to build a daily habit.

The Approach

Conducted user interviews with novice investors to understand their fears and motivations. Designed a gamified trading experience with daily contests, social leaderboards, and real-time market data. Rapid prototyping and usability testing helped us nail the balance between fun and educational.

Key Decisions

Chose a dark UI to signal seriousness while using bold green accents to celebrate wins. Introduced a "Daily Contest" mechanic that created urgency and repeat engagement. Simplified chart interactions to three core gestures.
